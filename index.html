About

Hi, I'm Rob - a researcher and experience designer based in the lower North Island of New Zealand. I'm passionate about creating accessible, user-centered designs that make a real difference in people's lives.

With a strong grasp of design trends and an empathetic approach, I work to understand how users think, feel, and interact - designing experiences that truly meet their needs.

My journey began in multimedia with a love for 3D animation, eventually leading me to UX theory and design. While working in the public sector, I conducted user research and helped shape accessible solutions across a range of projects.

Today, I bring together skills in UX/UI design, visual design, accessibility, animation, and front-end development to deliver thoughtful, end-to-end design solutions.

a11y Guidance

Accessibility UI Front-end

The foundation of the accessibility (a11y) guidance originated from an internal project at the Ministry of Social Development. Initially developed to support internal project managers, content creators, designers, and developers, it was later adapted and enhanced for public users.

The guide outlines existing standards and provides helpful guidance to ensure all features meet accessibility (WCAG) standards.

Ministry of Social Development

UX Prototyping

The Single Client View project was a substantial endeavor aimed at consolidating multiple software systems into a unified platform for staff at Work and Income offices.

As part of a larger team, I extracted valuable insights from user research and low-fidelity wireframe testing. These insights informed the development of high-fidelity prototypes, which were then tested and refined based on feedback from New Zealand Work and Income staff members.

A generic light mode and the dark view were designed for testing environmental lighting conditions.

UX Visual Design Prototyping
